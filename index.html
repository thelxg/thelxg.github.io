Daaaaamn Homey, you spent a lot on RBs!

The biggest take-away from the auction has got to be the inflation we saw at the RB position across the top 10. Lordy lordy those were some big numbas. Barkley for $69 (nice) was pretty expected, but then we turn around and see Kamara also at $69 (nice) and the market was set. The next RBs then went for 65, 64, and 56 before we saw some reasonable discounts on DJ for 46 and Mixon for 40. High end WRs topped out at 53, 48, 48, 44, 43, 43, before we saw a drop off to 35 for Evans. In sum, the top of the markets formed a plateau where guys were virtually valued the same followed by a cascading drop off from there. Nobody had excess money this year, as opposed to 3-4 people with wasted money last year, that’s a really impressive skill jump in just one year.

Power. Fucking. Ranks.

This year I kept separate the two forms of power ranks and give them each their own section. As always, I'll give you my takes on the teams as well for extra color and to remind you all how big my dick is.

Automated Ranks

You: WHAT ARE THESE NUMBERS!?

Me: These automated ranks came from 3 different internet surf-zones: 1. Football Guys draft reports cards, 2. FantasyPros Power Ranks, 3. ESPN projections

You: BUT CAN'T YOU MAKE NUMBERS SAY ANYTHING YOU WANT?!?

Me: Obviously, and that's why science and specifically climate evolution change are a China lie, but in this case NO! these numbers are objective as fuck. The Football guys system kicks back an auto-generated report which gives your chances of making the playoffs. Three numbers are given, %chance with "great" management, "good" management, and "average" management. I use the "average" number in all cases. The logic is simple, the league is too good for anybody to perform better than "average" in the context of the league, a solid 7 of us make all the right waiver claims for our needs and the right starting rosters almost every week. Football Guys gives you these %chance values based on 4 of their experts ranks, so your Football Guys score is an average of the 4.

You: WOW! I'M NOT AVERAGE I'M GREAT! YOU'RE BAD! WHAT ABOUT FANTASY PROS!?!?!?

Me: Fantasy Pros generates power ranks based on the 20 most accurate fantasy expert predictors from last year, this yields a 0-100 score.

You: WHAT ABOUT ESPN!!?! I HATE ESPN PREDICTIONS AAHHHAJDSJDHV!!!

Me: Since Mike Clay was hired ESPNs season long predictions have been better, the weekly is still questionable. For the ESPN score, I added together your starting roster projected points, I then added your bench points together and reduced them by %60. The logic here is that ideally, in a season without injuries or busts, your bench will hardly see the field, so they shouldn't count as fully as your starters. This advantaged 7/10 teams in the league who have virtually no projected bench points. The calculation was Starter Points + (Bench Points x .4) = Total

You: WHY ARE ALL THE NUMBERS NEAR 50!?!?

Me: Great question! I standardized all the scores so that they could be meaningfully compared to each other! To start each metric used a wildly different scale (%chance, 0-100 power number, 1500+ projected points), adding them together would be a disaster. In the past I've simply computed your rank on each metric and averages your rank. This is a decent enough system but you loose all of your effect size data. In other words, you don't know how much better team 1 is than team 2 and how much better team 5 is than team 10. By standardizing the scores you get a sense for just how much different the teams are. (For Rosen, I computed Zscores for each metric then converted them to Tscores [WAIS style] for ease of viewing).

All of these was pulled before the Hyde/McCoy news of late, I imagine there's a very slight uptick for both Jazz Cabbage and Dom Vio (Singletary pickup, McCoy's new spot), Duke has not moved in the ranks as of my viewing today. Also imagine there would have been a very slight uptick for Adorable Pups with the Brisset (seriously WTF was that pick) for John Brown move. Likely none of that moves the following conclusions.

Yes, the computers like me. You probably have caught on this happens most years. Its because I spend a lot of time reading about fantasy football so the fantasy football industry is going to generally like what I've done. (I should note, none of these sources are where I pull my ranks, so my team hasn't been taught to the test). Interestingly, NVRB had the lowest projected starter points, but the highest projected bench points to such a degree that even when reduced by 60% it boasted the overall projection to the top (by a fairly large margin). I would interpret this to mean something that you likely found when rating this team, the ceiling of the projected outcome doesn't stick out as top-end, but the depth ought to represent a pretty high floor.

You'll notice there's a fair bit of variation. The most obvious source of variation is the experts evaluation of your players. Additionally, you'll notice some site-wide variation around how important various positions were weighted. It seems Football Guyz strongly prefers you have 2 beast RBS, and those that do were bumped up, FantasyPros weights the positions more equally meaning those with a strong Flex were rewarded, ESPN is more even although it possibly weighs the bench a bit more despite the deliberate devaluation of bench production in the calculation. In aggregate I these numbers do point to some wild fluctuation in the quality of both Flex options and bench depth.

Also of note, Football Guys hated Hand Grenades and Jazz Cabbage to a level I had never seen before, getting single digit %chance to make playoffs from several experts (average was 20-40%).

Fuck Drew for picking these divisions.

Peer Rankings

The owner of the Hand Grenades has apparently been super busy, too busy for me to get a 1-10 list from him, so we have 8 raters this year (we no longer ask the Pups as he always gives back a randomly ordered list). I will discuss the teams in the order they appeared on the draft board. As always I removed everyone’s self-rank because you all think you’re top 3 because *surprise!* you all like all your own players and think they’re the best!

Bukkake; Automated Rank: 5 Peer Rank: 5 My Rank: 4

Not a lot of disagreement here. We all think this is a pretty solid roster. More balanced than most of the rosters, and one of 4 teams that has legit WR depth. The lack of any none-lottery ticket RBs is a concern, especially given how small McCaffery and Lindsey are and Sony’s knee troubles. Beckham, Cooper, and Alshon are also notorious for coming up lame. (FUN FACT! Odell Beckham has missed more games than Sammy Watkins! They entered the league in the same year). As much as any team can be truly injury prone, this is the one that sticks out, even Anderson and Jones have had troubles recently. If they all stay healthy the talent is here, the big weeks should be big, but that WR depth may be needed sooner than later.

“I like Bukkakes Rbs, wilson is always a pretty solid pick. His wrs as long as healthy with be productive. He has strong enough flex otherwise to contend every week.” -A person who isn’t me

Rape Babies; Automated Rank: 1 Peer Rank: T2 My Rank: N/A

I didn’t rank me because I’m also not free from bias. That said, its not hard to evaluate this team. Mixon’s offense likely pulls down his upside as compared to the other top10 backs, there is workload but possible not enough blow-up games. Fournette has been unstable, Ingram is a strong flex compared to nearly every other roster and is the real path forward here. Evans is kind of the Mixon of WRs, Diggs is an average and oft injured WR2, Lockett is one of the best WR3s in the league. QB and TE exist and are fine. The bench is the strongest in the league, but that may not matter if nobody pops enough to win matchups. The deficits of this roster is at its peaks, and its major strengths are the RB3 and WR3 positions where this team will need to majorly make-up ground.

Necros; Automated Rank: 2 Peer Rank: 1 My Rank: 1

Shawn is the big bad wolf coming into the season. WRs are better than I think a lot of people realize, the consistency of Hopkins and Adams is just going to create unfair matchups this year. Ridley/Boyd is better than all but one teams WR3 situation. RBs, while they should be a weakness, I see as average. Especially if Carson and Montgomery live up to the hype. The real concern for me is that Latavius as an RB4 is pretty low-upside, Penny and Brieda (because of Go-Rape-a-Hoe) are trending down. QB/TE are fine.

Adorable Puppies; Automated Rank: 8 Peer Rank: 8 My Rank: 8

We all agree! Except two of you who put him 2 and 3. I assume their logic is “last year all he needed was 2 dope RBs and playoffs!” Those people forget he hit on 3 WRs last year in Cooper, Woods, and Lockett all for basically free. That doesn’t mean he couldn’t do it again, but it does mean that the reason he made the playoffs was not simply “2 dope RBs nothing else.” The rest of us have rated the team accordingly. Having the #1 and #2 players in fantasy is pretty cool, those guys should be great. But RB3 is a joke and RB4 is worse. The WRs are closer to average than they should be thanks to a good deal on Gordon, but none of these 3 guys are what I would describe as safe. The bench is a wasteland. Even if the 2 studs stay healthy I don’t love this team, if Saquon can’t repeat his production in NY or either gets hurt this thing is gonna be ugly as fuck.

Domestic Violence; Automated Rank: 4 Peer Rank: T2 My Rank: 2

The stars shine bright at each position, and you can tell that’s where ALL the money went. This is stars and scrubs to a ridiculous extent. The mid caliber players are Jacobs, and Woods, who should be solid and were decent values. The bright spot of Bell didn’t seem worth the cost of having McCoy at RB3, before the move to KC, and there’s nothing at all at RB4. Julio, Allen, Woods are strong and should be a weekly advantage against all but a couple teams, but WR4 and below are non-existent. Mahomes and Kittle should be weekly advantages even with some regression.

“DDV has the balance of having an elite RB, elite QB, and an Elite WR, which, if they remain healthy should be able to carry them with some decent waiver management.” -Twas not I

Hand Grenades; Automated Rank: 6 Peer Rank: 7 My Rank: 9

This team absolutely has to start hot. The big knock on both Chubb and Gurley this year was that you cannot count on them down the stretch. Winning now is more important than winning later, so the strategy itself at RB isn’t a problem, but the rest of the roster is incredibly ill-suited for that goal. Cooks was my WR3 last year, and he was a mismatch all year in that role, he’s not who you necessarily want at WR1. I’m gonna fade Edelman every year until he’s out of the league. Fuller is a WR4 that he has to start every week. TE is average. Guice, Drake and Henderson (handcuff) are not bad depth at RB, but the WR depth is a fucking joke. I don’t expect this team to be better than his team last year in the long run even if he starts hot.

Hot Pockets; Automated Rank: 3 Peer Rank: 6 My Rank: 5

If you like Henry and Damian Williams then 5 will seem low, if you remember how Henry played through the first 12 weeks and that Williams is a journeyman (who is now dealing with McCoy), 5 is about right. At best this is three RB2s with depth behind them, at worst its 15 combined points a week. WR1 and 2 are strong, and Pettis is probably a solid enough WR3. The depth WRs are lottery tickets but at least they could hit big with the right situation, so I’m not mad at it. Watson and Ertz should continue to be an advantage. In aggregate this team ends up better than most, but if waiver claims need be made, color be concerned.

Injury optimism is a trap (remember Baldwin last year?) and holdouts are unpredictable AF. Godwin is a WR3 cast as a WR1, Landry is a lowend WR3 cast as a WR2, Gallup is a WR5 cast as a WR3.
